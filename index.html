How Its All Connected: The Hidden World of Human Trafficking

Some general stats for human trafficking before we get started!

1) Human trafficking is modern day slavery that involves the use of force in sex trafficking, forced labor or domestic servitude.

2) Sex trafficking vs. labor trafficking:

- Sex trafficking--crime of using force to induce individuals into selling sex for illicit pornography, massage businesses, brothels, escort services, and outdoor solicitation

- Labor trafficking--crime of using force to induce individuals to provide service and work (ex. Working in agriculture, domestic work, healthcare, trucking, etc.)

3) Traffickers make up to 9.5 billion a year in the sex trafficking business

4) The AMP Model: Action-Means-Purpose is a way to understand the federal law when it comes to human trafficking

- The Action: a perpetrator employing a mean of force, coercion, and fraud for the Purpose of bringing the victim to do commerical sex labor, acts, and service

5) Human traffick victims are more targetted if they are homeless or if they are current victims of a situation already

6) Trafficking also comes from a place of false sense of comfort--which is why they also target young people, minorities, POC, LGBTQIA+ people, and more

7) Young women can often be forced into prostitution, and can be confused as a valid sex worker.

8) Traffickers use fraud, coercion, and force to lure their victims into exploitation; they also use grooming, which is forcing minors to believe that making sexual decisions at a young age by themselves is normal and healthy.

9) Trafficking happens all around the world, even in places you wouldn’t consider a dangerous place.

10) Men are often used for forced labor, while women are used for sexual exploitation

11) 30 million victims are 18 and older and 10 million victims are under 18.

12) 83 percent of victims being female, 16 percent male, and one percent gender minority
